1 step one

Meet the graph machine

An like y = x ÷ 2 + 1 is a little machine: feed it a number (that's x), and it answers with a number (that's y). Each answer gets drawn as a dot at . Drag the slider and watch it work.

The one idea everything else builds on

A graph isn't the machine — it's the machine's diary. Hit "feed it every number" and the dots melt into a line: every input the machine ever met, with its answer, drawn as one picture.

2 step two · the green knob

Slide it up & down

Mess with the number at the end and every answer grows or shrinks by exactly that much. The line doesn't tilt, doesn't bend — it rides the elevator. The arrows show every dot taking the same trip.

no tricks here

This knob behaves exactly how you'd guess: bigger number at the end, higher graph. Enjoy it — the next knob is sneakier.

3 step three · the blue knob

Slide it sideways — the sneaky one

Now we mess with x itself, before the machine gets it: y = |x − 3| − 2. Subtracting 3 slides the whole picture… to the right?! Almost everyone guesses left. Here's the click: x − 3 is a delay. The machine now treats every input as 3 smaller than it really is, so whatever used to happen at 0 now happens at 3.

opposite-day rule

Inside the parentheses, everything is opposite-day. Minus slides the graph right. Plus slides it left. Outside (the green knob), things behave normally.

Watch the copycat race

Still feels like a trick? Two pens draw at the same time, left to right. The blue pen copies the black pen's every move — three steps late.

why we used a V and not a line

Try sliding a plain line sideways and it looks like it just slid down — a line has no corner to track, so both stories are true at once. The V's corner makes the sideways move impossible to miss.

4 step four · the orange knob

Stretch, squish & flip

The last knob multiplies every answer. Crank it to 2 and every height doubles — the curve looks taller and skinnier. Drop it to ½ and it flattens out, lazy and wide. Drag it below zero and every answer flips sign: the whole graph does a somersault.

5 step five · all three knobs

Every shape obeys the same knobs

Lines, U-curves, S-curves, V-shapes, waves — every graph ever drawn follows the same three knobs: orange stretches, blue slides sideways (opposite-day!), green rides the elevator. Pick a shape and go wild — or just grab the curve and drag it around.
